# Menstrual Health Medical Reference Standards

## Normal Menstrual Cycle Parameters

| Indicator | Normal Range | Needs Attention | Recommend Medical Visit |
|-----------|-------------|-----------------|------------------------|
| Cycle Length | 21-35 days | <21 or >35 days | <21 days for 3+ cycles |
| Period Duration | 3-7 days | <2 or >8 days | <2 or >10 days |
| Blood Volume | 20-80ml | <5ml or >80ml | Soaking through pad >1/hr |
| Cycle Variability | Within ±7 days | ±7-14 days | >14 day variation |
| Cramp Severity | Mild, tolerable | Affects daily life | Bedridden or painkillers ineffective |
| Ovulation Day | 14 days before next | Irregular | Sustained anovulation |

## Menstrual Cycle Phases & Hormonal Changes

### Menstrual Phase (Day 1-5)
- Estrogen and progesterone at lowest
- Endometrial shedding
- Prone to fatigue, mood swings
- Iron loss

### Follicular Phase (Day 6-13)
- Estrogen gradually rising
- Energy recovering
- Skin condition improving
- Metabolic rate rising

### Ovulation (Around Day 14)
- LH surge triggers ovulation
- BBT rises 0.3-0.5°C
- Increased libido
- Some experience ovulation pain

### Luteal Phase (Day 15-28)
- Progesterone dominant
- Possible PMS symptoms: low mood, bloating, breast tenderness, appetite changes
- BBT remains elevated
- Temperature drops 1-2 days before period

## Common Symptom Categories

### Physical Symptoms
- Abdominal pain/cramps
- Lower back pain
- Headache
- Breast tenderness
- Bloating
- Fatigue
- Nausea
- Diarrhea/constipation
- Acne breakout
- Appetite changes
- Insomnia

### Emotional Symptoms
- Irritability
- Anxiety
- Low mood
- Mood swings
- Poor concentration
- Crying spells
- Social withdrawal

### Physical Signs
- Basal body temperature (BBT)
- Weight changes
- Cervical mucus observation
- Exercise performance
- Sleep quality
- Libido changes

## Influencing Factors (Variables That May Affect Cycles)

### Lifestyle
- Sleep deprivation (<6 hrs/day)
- Intense exercise changes
- Travel/jet lag
- Major dietary changes
- Significant weight changes (±5kg/month)
- Alcohol/caffeine intake

### Psychological
- High-stress events (exams, deadlines, interpersonal conflict)
- Chronic anxiety/depression
- Traumatic experiences
- Major life changes (moving, job change)

### Physiological
- Illness/fever
- Medication changes (antibiotics, hormonal medications)
- Vaccination
- Breastfeeding
- Underlying conditions (PCOS, endometriosis, etc.)

### Environmental
- Seasonal changes
- Pollution/environmental toxin exposure
- Work environment changes

## Nutrition Recommendations

### Pre-Menstrual (Late Luteal Phase)
- Magnesium: dark chocolate, nuts, dark leafy greens
- Vitamin B6: bananas, chicken, potatoes
- Reduce salt: alleviate bloating
- Complex carbs: stabilize blood sugar and mood

### During Menstruation
- Iron: red meat, spinach, black fungus, red dates
- Vitamin C: enhance iron absorption (citrus, kiwi)
- Warm foods: ginger tea, brown sugar water
- Avoid cold/raw foods: reduce cramping

### Post-Menstrual (Follicular Phase)
- Protein: tissue repair
- Omega-3: anti-inflammatory (deep-sea fish, flaxseed)
- Folate: key period for TTC

### Ovulation
- Zinc: support ovulation (oysters, pumpkin seeds)
- Vitamin E: endometrial health
- Adequate hydration

## Exercise Recommendations

| Phase | Recommended Exercise | Notes |
|-------|---------------------|-------|
| Menstrual Day 1-3 | Walking, yoga, stretching | Avoid high intensity, inverted poses |
| Menstrual Day 4-7 | Jogging, Pilates | Gradual return |
| Follicular Phase | HIIT, strength training | Peak performance window |
| Ovulation | Cardio, dance | Watch joint protection |
| Early Luteal Phase | Moderate intensity | Maintain exercise habit |
| Late Luteal Phase | Yoga, swimming, walking | Reduce intensity during PMS |

## Warning Signals (Recommend Medical Consultation)

1. Cycle <21 days or >35 days for 3+ consecutive months
2. Period >8 days or <2 days
3. Abnormally heavy flow (soaking through pad within 1 hour)
4. Non-menstrual bleeding
5. Severe dysmenorrhea affecting daily life
6. No period for over 3 months (not pregnant)
7. Menopause symptoms before age 45
8. Persistent post-menstrual bleeding
9. Abnormal discharge with odor
10. Post-coital bleeding
